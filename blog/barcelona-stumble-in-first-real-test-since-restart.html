Barcelona Stumble In First Real Test Since Restart

by Charlie Tuley - June 19, 2020

ctuley@umich.edu source: Twitter


Final Score: Sevilla 0 - 0 Barcelona

Barcelona failed to score in a goalless draw with Sevilla in their first true test after the Covid-19 break, a huge win for Sevilla in their pursuit of Champions League qualification.


1. Setien Chooses Braithwaite and Suarez Over Griezmann, Fati

In Barcelona’s first big match since the restart, Quique Setien opted for a now-fit Luis Suarez, along with the recently signed Martin Braithwaite to partner Lionel Messi in a front three. These two were chosen over the in-form Ansu Fati, who scored the match-winner against Leganes on Tuesday, and World Cup winner Antione Griezman, who had started both of Barcelona’s matches since the restart. Setien seems to favor Braithwaite, giving him the starting spot on the left wing in two of Barcelona’s last three matches. This is curious as the 29-year-old bagged his first goal for the club last Saturday against Mallorca, while Griezman is a much more prolific goalscorer with 8 goals for Los Blaugranes. Perhaps he believes that Braithwaite plays better as a winger than Griezman, as Griezman is typically an out and out striker? Barcelona fans also may have reason to be concerned, as Setien didn’t even bring on Fati in the match, and only used three of his five possible substitutes.


2. Messi Leaves the Ramón Sánchez-Pizjuán Without Goal 700

After securing his 699th career goal on Tuesday night against Leganes, all of the Barcelona faithful believed that Messi would hit the 700 mark on Friday night. After all, Sevilla is historically Messi’s favorite opponent, as he has netted 29 goals against them in his career. Sadly, Messi did not score his landmark goal last night, and he will have to wait for their match against Athletic Club at the Camp Nou on Tuesday.


3. Sevilla Defense Put in Great Performance

Sevilla’s defense, who have performed very well this season (conceding 30 goals in 29 games), fought hard and kept a clean sheet against a veteran Barcelona attack on Friday night. Center backs Diego Carlos and Jules Koundé locked down Luis Suarez and Lionel Messi, while wing-backs Jesus Navas and Sergio Reguilon defended Jordi Alba, Antione Griezman, Martin Braithwaite and Nelson Semedo valiantly. Additionally, goalkeeper Tomáš Vaclík had 3 key saves, including one to tip a Messi free-kick over the bar. Sevilla will need their defense to continue to show up if they want to qualify for a Champions League position, as they currently sit in third place in La Liga, five points above fifth-placed Getafe.


4. Messi Clashes With Diego Carlos, Escapes Without a Card

During injury time in the first half, Jules Koundé fouled Luis Suarez and gave up a free-kick deep in Sevilla’s territory. All of the players began to gather and a heated exchange ensued. Amid the chaos, Messi, who had traded blows and words with Diego Carlos throughout the first half, seemed to hit Carlos in the face. Carlos obviously exaggerated the extent of his injury, to try and convince the referee to card Messi. However, the yellow card was only shown to Sergio Busquets and Fernando Reges, who had a smaller quarrel during the melee. This just continues the trend of bias from referees to La Liga’s two biggest clubs, and in extent, Messi himself. Sevilla will feel hard done by after Messi was allowed to continue without any sort of caution from the referee, as this easily could have been a red card offense. source: Twitter



5. Real Madrid Now Have Advantage In La Liga Race

After last night’s slip, Real Madrid will have the opportunity to go top of La Liga if they beat Real Sociedad Sunday night, due to their superior head-to-head record against Barcelona. Los Blaugranes will have to hope that La Real will beat Real Madrid in their home stadium if Barcelona wants any chance of winning La Liga. On paper, Sociedad looks to be Madrid’s toughest match for the rest of the season, so Barcelona will have to pray that fortune favors the Blue and Whites on Sunday. source: AP
