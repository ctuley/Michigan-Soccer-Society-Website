Manchester United Best Spurs in Five-Goal Thriller

by Gordon Lewis - March 12, 2022

gordonrl@umich.edu


Final Score: Manchester United 3 - 2 Tottenham

Either side could have emerged victorious from this five-goal thriller at Old Trafford, but Ralf Ragnick’s men came out on top. A hat-trick from Cristiano Ronaldo canceled out a confident Harry Kane penalty and an own goal from Harry Maguire. Neither Manchester United nor Tottenham are in the driver’s seat for the Premier League’s fourth spot, but this still stands as a big win for the Red Devils.


1. The job is far from done for United.

After today’s win, Manchester United finds themselves in the coveted fourth-place spot, standing two points ahead of their nearest rivals, Arsenal, and five ahead of the likes of Tottenham and West Ham — but they have also played two more games than Spurs and a whopping four more than the Gunners. The Red Devils will have to dig deep and finally find some consistency if they are to keep their place. The work is far from over for Ralf Ragnick’s men.


2. Hats off to Ronaldo.

Ronaldo has faced a lot of criticism upon returning to United, and most of it has been justified, but everyone should bow down and kiss his feet after his performance today. The thirty-seven-year-old from Portugal scored a hat-trick of stunners to sink Tottenham and broke FIFA’s all-time goal-scoring record in the process (807 goals at the time of writing). Yes, he may offer little defensively and cost the Glazer family nearly 100,000 Chipotle burritos (about $625,000) per week, but he remains unstoppable on his day. source: Ash donelon/Manchester United via Getty Images


3. Inconsistency killed the Red Devil.

A theme of the previous two points has been the inconsistency of Manchester United. Today, nearly every player on the red side of the pitch looked confident and controlled, and they never gave up despite being twice pegged back by the Englishmen named Harry (Kane and Maguire). A performance like this makes it hard to believe that the same team tied relegation candidates Watford 0 - 0 and were bullied by their cross-town rivals Manchester City. United have the tools to finish in fourth-place — the likes of Pogba, Ronaldo, and their entire back four just need to stay fit and in form for more than one game.


4. The fourth spot might be too far for Spurs now.

Tottenham was neck and neck with their opponents for the entire game, forcing Ronaldo to roll back the years, but at the end of the day they still lost and they are still trailing a trio of strong teams. Antonio Conte’s men have unfortunately fallen to the same inconsistency bug as many of their nearby rivals and, even with a string of good form, it could very well be too late for them now. Maybe Conte’s system will come to full force next season (assuming he and Harry Kane stay), but this season will probably go down as another disappointment for the London side. Tottenham is still strong, but the teams above them are stronger.


5. Summer departures for Spurs?

This is a rather speculative point but is something to consider nonetheless. Despite the respectable performance, Antonio Conte will be angry, and an angry Conte never lasts. The hot-headed Italian trophy-winner left Inter Milan and Chelsea because of disagreements with the respective administrations so it is always a possibility if he doesn’t get what he wants before next season begins. Furthermore, stars like Harry Kane will not be happy with yet another disappointing season. He wanted to go last summer and it’s hard to see him changing his mind. If the star men at Tottenham do leave then trophies may still be a long way away.
