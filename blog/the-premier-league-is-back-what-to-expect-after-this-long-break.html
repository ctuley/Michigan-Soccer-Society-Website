The Premier League is Back! What to Expect After this Long Break

by Lucas Kellersohn - June 14, 2020

kellersl@umich.edu


After more than three months of waiting, we are less than a week away from the return of the Premier League. However, playing football during a global pandemic comes with some challenges, most evident in the lack of fans present. Before the first kick off, let’s examine some of the new norms we can expect, and how these will affect the rest of the season.


As seen in the return of the Bundesliga so far, the atmosphere is completely different on the pitch, causing matches to be less momentum based. Due to these changes in atmosphere, I expect that the talent gap will be accentuated, leading to fewer upsets from the lower standing clubs. Having to play away from home is hardly a disadvantage without fans shifting the tides of the game. In fact, according to an article from ESPN’s Tom Hamilton, home win percentages in the Bundesliga have dropped from 43.3% to 21.7% since the return. These empty stadiums are proving to level the playing field, and I expect the majority of outcomes to be in favor of the better side. The top three teams of Liverpool, Manchester City, and Leicester City should find themselves winning out most of their games against the clubs in the bottom half of the table. Upsets are bound to happen as these times are currently more unpredictable than ever, but the lack of a home field advantage will most likely favor the more talented side.


With that being said, it can be almost guaranteed that Liverpool will win their first Premier League title this year. With a 25 point lead and coming off a 6-0 battering of Blackburn in a club friendly this week, it seems that they are ready to finish the job this year. It will be a tad bittersweet having no fans to celebrate in front of, but after 30 years of waiting, I’m sure Klopp and his boys will be overjoyed to lift that trophy. In addition to winning the league, Liverpool have the opportunity to break many records, such as Manchester United’s record of fewest games remaining when clinching the title (5 games) or Manchester City’s record of 100 points in a season. Having been knocked out of the Champions League before the hiatus, Liverpool will have their full focus set on winning the league, and it will be fun to watch this squad finish their season.


Another rule change that will affect the return of the season comes in the form of substitution reform. For the remainder of the season, teams will be allowed benches of nine players and up to five substitutions as opposed to the usual three per game. These new substitution rules will allow for teams to completely change their shape and play styles mid game and will really benefit teams who have deep and talented benches. Clubs like Chelsea and Manchester City should benefit highly from these additional substitutions with their depth and young talent. Chelsea’s Billy Gilmour, Christian Pulisic, and Callum Hudson-Odoi will likely see the pitch more frequently, possibly allowing them to prove their talent and earn a transfer or a more permanent spot in the lineup. In addition to the young talent on show, it will be interesting to see how well recent transfers such as Manchester United’s Bruno Fernandes and Tottenham’s Steven Bergwijn fit into their squads, after hot starts in their first five appearances.


The last expectation I have for the return of the season is a top seven battle for the ages. With Manchester City’s Champions League ban in place and Liverpool’s spot secured, the rest of the teams will be left to battle it out for their chance at European football next season. Only 13 points separate third place Leicester from ninth place Arsenal, and with seven matches to go and some high tier matchups, the fight is on for these clubs. Newly promoted Sheffield United looks poised for a Europa League ticket if they can hang on. Additionally, Arsenal and Tottenham are currently on the outside looking in, just three and two points away respectively from their chance at returning to European football. With less than 10 matches remaining for the London clubs, it is crunch time.


In these uncertain times, having football back in action is something that we can all look forward to and celebrate together. Kicking off Wednesday June 17th with two matches and continuing on a near daily basis after, there is plenty to keep you occupied for the remainder of the summer. Whether your club is fighting relegation, earning their spot to play in Europe next season, or even if you have no stakes in the league other than loving the game, there is action for everyone to enjoy with its return.
