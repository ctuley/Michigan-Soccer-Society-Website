Villarreal Shock Manchester United In Penalty Shootout

by Avi Rajendra-Nicolucci - May 27, 2021

| avirn@umich.edu source: The Athletic


Final Score: Manchester United (10 0-0 (11) Villarreal

The Europa League final was a nervy match typical of cup finals. Villarreal grabbed the lead from a set-piece delivery from Dani Parejo in the 29th minute. Striker Gerard Moreno got the slightest of touches from the whipped free-kick sending the ball past Manchester United’s David de Gea. United equalized in the 55th minute through Edinson Cavani after a ricochet gave the Uruguayan a golden opportunity. The penalty shootout finished 11-10 with the sole miss coming from de Gea. It was a disappointing end to the season for United and a glorious achievement from Villarreal who punched above their financial weight to win their first major trophy. Here are five key takeaways from the match:


1. Manchester United Still Struggle In Possession

It has become well known that United prefer to play on the counter-attack rather than dominate possession. The mid-low 4-4-2 block of Villarreal vexed United’s attackers who couldn’t find a way through the middle and resorted to hopeful crosses from the wing. It was still surprising to see Manchester United look like they were out of ideas considering they face similar challenges in the Premier League against lower table opponents. With over 60% of possession United only mustered two shots on target. Villarreal seemed out of the match after United equalized but they actually managed only two fewer shots than the Red Devils. The expected goals tally was 0.99 - 0.92 in favor of United, but not a margin to be proud of.

source: CBS Sports


2. Unai Emery Gets His Revenge

Villarreal manager Unai Emery was widely mocked in England after his unsuccessful tenure at Arsenal. This victory and the entire cup run showed his prowess as a manager even as they finished 7th in La Liga this year. He scored victories over Manchester United, Arsenal and RB Salzburg throughout the campaign. The win qualifies Villarreal for the Champions League next season and may even propel Emery to a higher profile job this summer.



3. Knockout Football Is A Different Beast

League games and knockout ties feel like different sports. In a win-or-go-home situation nothing matters except the result. Therefore, style can be abandoned without criticism. Which leads to cynical tactics like we saw from Villarreal who averaged 55% possession in La Liga. It was a smart play by Emery who knew that United would struggle to be effective without being able to counter attack. Ole Gunnar Solskjaer stuck with his starting eleven until extra time when he put Fred in for Mason Greenwood in the 100th minute. This would be unheard of in a league game but Ole clearly had a group of players which he trusted.


4. Penalty Shootout Blunders
source: The Independent


United won the coin toss and elected to shoot second. In penalty shootouts the team which shoots first wins 60% of the time. Bruno Fernandes made the call but this is something that the coaching staff should’ve communicated to him about. If they intentionally chose to shoot second...well that would be a typical Manchester United move. Also, de Gea reportedly had notes on where Villarreal players shoot but he chose to disregard them and go off his own instinct. De Gea is below average at saving penalties and Solskjaer probably should’ve brought on backup Dean Henderson who has a better record in this situation.



5. Spanish Dominance Continues

A team from La Liga has won the Europa League six out of the past eight times. English teams won twice in the same span. La Liga and the Premier League are widely regarded as the top two leagues in Europe, but when it comes to performance in finals the Spanish teams seem to get the job done. The Spanish have also won five out of the last seven Champions Leagues. I think generally English teams and the media tend to underestimate their European opponents if they are not a Real Madrid or Barcelona. Villarreal showed the depth of La Liga and an impressive tactical flexibility on their run to the title.
