Victory Against NYCFC Just the Start for the Philadelphia Union

by Jacob Cohen - July 10, 2020

| jacobcoh@umich.edu source: Douglas P. DeFelice/Getty Images


Final Score: NYCFC 0 - 1 Philadelphia Union

The Philadelphia Union defeated NYCFC 1-0 in the opening weekend of the MLS is Back Tournament, thanks to a second-half goal from Alejandro Bedoya. Here are the five key takeaways:


1. Alejandro Bedoya is Mr. Reliable

Arguably the most experienced player on the pitch - having played club football in Sweden, Scotland, and France and featured regularly for the USMNT - Alejandro Bedoya made the difference between the Union and NYCFC. The Philadelphia captain scored in the 63rd minute with a tidy finish from a tough angle, logging the first and only goal of the match. Although the midfielder does not score much, he came through when his side needed him most. In a knockout-style tournament, moments like that which Bedoya produced are crucial, and the Union have him to thank as they push to finish top of their group.


2. NYCFC Rusty in Front of Goal

In 90 minutes of football, NYCFC recorded 18 shots, 9 of which were on target—for those who are not aware, that is a lot of shots. Despite their exciting attacking display, however, forwards Valentín Castellanos, Héber, and Ismael Tajouri-Shradi could not find the back of the net. While it was an unfortunate outcome, NYCFC should be encouraged by the number of shots they were able to get off. If the squad can shake the cobwebs off, it can do some real damage in the remainder of the tournament.


3. Andre Blake Keeps a Hard-Earned Clean Sheet

Nothing should be taken away from the performance of Andre Blake, the Union’s superstar goalkeeper. The Jamaican international logged 7 saves and an 8.0-rated man-of-the-match performance, according to WhoScored. Possibly the most dramatic moment of the match came in the 86’ when Blake, fully outstretched, made a fingertip save from point-blank range preserving the clean sheet. Blake is entering his seventh season with Philadelphia and his display against NYCFC has strengthened his case as one of the top goalkeepers in the MLS. source: AP Photo


4. Union Youth is the Real Deal

The Philadelphia Union has received lots of attention this year over the number of young players that have filled the squad. Prospects including Brenden Aaronson, Mark McKenzie, and Anthony Fontana—all U21 and featuring in the match against NYCFC—are showing great promise for Jim Curtin’s side. In a hard-fought match, they all helped keep a clean sheet while Aaronson was able to offer some extra flair going forward. Aaronson, McKenzie, Fontana, as well as left-back Matt Real, all graduated from the Union Academy and have been representing the club with great success. At this rate, it is only a matter of time before European teams come swooping in to bring the young Americans abroad.


5. Philadelphia a Dark Horse for the Tournament?

The media favorites for the MLS is Back Tournament are LAFC, Seattle Sounders, or Atlanta United—but the Philadelphia Union have forced themselves into the conversation with a successful campaign last season and a win against NYCFC, who were picked by many to win Group A. Jim Curtin has been with the Union for 5 seasons and has finished each season with more points than the last. It could finally happen for the club, with an exciting combination of experienced players such as Alejandro Bedoya and Ilsinho, and Andre Blake, and homegrown talent in Brenden Aaronson and Mark McKenzie. Kacper Przybyłko, who is coming into his own as a striker having scored 15 goals last season, is the perfect man to lead the line. Keep an eye on Philadelphia throughout this tournament and beyond. source: Andrew Zwarych / Philadelphia Union
