Real Madrid Overcome Getafe in Grueling Win

by Charlie Tuley - July 2, 2020

ctuley@umich.edu source: Twitter/@realmadrid


Final Score: Real Madrid 1 - 0 Getafe

Real Madrid needed a Sergio Ramos penalty to beat Getafe in what was an intense matchup.


1. Isco Gets Consecutive Starts, Disappoints Again

Isco made his 13th start of the La Liga season Thursday night, his second since the restart. Again, Zidane deployed him at the head of a midfield diamond, and again, it didn’t work too well. Getafe’s high-pressure system restricted how often Isco could get on the ball, and how much he could do with the ball when on it. He could not show off his creativity due to the suffocation of him and his teammates that gave them no time on the ball. Isco should not have started this match, and Zidane should’ve utilized a winger like Rodrygo or Asensio instead.


2. Getafe’s Press Proves Troublesome for Real Madrid

Getafe plays one of the toughest pressing systems in the world, with all 10 field players acting as defenders when they don’t have the ball. Their main strategy is to use wide midfielders Marc Cucurella and Allan Nyom to suffocate the opposition fullbacks into giving up the ball in their own defensive third. This pressuring system made life difficult for Madrid fullbacks Dani Carvajal and Ferland Mendy, who each gave up possession deep in their territory multiple times. Furthermore, the high-pressure system impacted Real Madrid goalkeeper Thibaut Courtois, who misplaced 2 passes while being suffocated by Getafe attackers. Madrid had to defend for long periods throughout the game because each time they would attempt to play out of the back Getafe would win the ball.


3. A Very Physical Game, Going Both Ways

Getafe is La Liga’s most physical team (they have the most yellow cards in the league this season) and are known for playing a hard-hitting brand of football. This is exemplified by midfielder David Timor getting a yellow card 12 seconds into the match. In total, the match had 37 fouls (15 by Real Madrid and 22 by Getafe) and 10 yellow cards (5 per team). The number of fouls greatly disrupted play, and it felt like there was very little rhythm to the match, which could explain the lack of goals.


4. Substitutes Earn Madrid the Win

In the 64th minute, Zidane made the changes that won his team the match. He substituted the underperformers Isco, Modric, and Vinicius Jr., for Rodrygo, Asensio, and Fede Valverde. While Fede didn’t have too much of an impact on the match, Asensio and Rodrygo brought a new dynamic to the team. Real Madrid began to use the full width of the pitch thanks to their recently brought-on wingers, and this change in dynamic gave Real Madrid their goal. Rodrygo got the ball to Carvajal on the right flank, who then dribbled into Getafe’s box, where he was brought down by left-back Mathías Olivera for a penalty. The penalty was slotted home by Sergio Ramos, and that proved to be the only difference between the teams. source: Twitter/@realmadrid



5. Four-Point Cushion Practically Ensures Real Madrid the Title

Real Madrid now has a 4 point lead on rivals Barcelona, and it looks like they will comfortably win the title this season. They have one more serious test when they play Athletic Club on Sunday, but they can lose that match and still have the lead. After that match, they will face Alaves, Granada, Villareal, and Leganes to finish La Liga. Judging by how Madrid have played recently, all of these games should earn them 3 points, and in the end, the title.
