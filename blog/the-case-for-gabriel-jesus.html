The Case for Gabriel Jesus

by Avi Rajendra-Nicolucci - May 13, 2020

| avirn@umich.edu


It is conventional thinking in the football world that Gabriel Jesus isn’t fit to replace Sergio Aguero as Manchester City’s striker. This kind of groupthink oozes from every corner of the football world. The disrespect towards Jesus has permeated newspapers and maybe even into Manchester City’s boardroom. City have been heavily linked with Lautaro Martinez while Juventus are rumored to be interested in signing Jesus.


Where this comes from is puzzling. If you look at the stats they suggest that Jesus is in fact up to the task. Pep Guardiola signed Jesus for a reason in 2017, he saw how well the Brazilian could fit into his system and the potential he had at only 19 years old. Jesus has had the benefit of working under Guardiola and being the understudy to Aguero for four seasons. If you look at their statistical profiles, Jesus and Aguero are very similar. Aguero moved to the Etihad when he was 23 years old and took the next step in his career to become an elite striker. Jesus is poised to do the same at essentially the same age.


Now back to the potential swap of Jesus and Martinez. Jesus is only four months older than Lautaro, but Jesus already has 43 more league appearances and 20 more league goals. Statistically, Jesus is one of the best young attackers in the world. His non-penalty xG are in the 99th percentile, his dribbling success, pass completion and assists are all in the 95th percentile or above. Jesus is a complete forward. In comparison, Lautaro has a promising profile but is not on the same level.


At this point, Jesus is further along in his development and deserves the opportunity to become the starting striker when Aguero leaves. Some have suggested that Martinez’s ceiling is higher, be that as it may, Manchester City don’t need to give up on Jesus to bring in Lautaro.


Manchester City’s activity in the transfer market usually focuses on experienced, proven, world class players or ones for the future. Martinez doesn’t quite fit into either category, but he’s closer to the former than the latter. If Lautaro is brought in to provide competition with Jesus I think it could be a sensible move, but I believe selling Jesus would be very unwise.


It’s not easy trying to be the heir to your club’s all time leading goalscorer. Gabriel Jesus has had to contend with the daunting statistical profile of Aguero and an emotional shadow looms large because of Aguero’s league-winning goal in 2012. For some City fans, Jesus will never be enough because he isn’t named Sergio Aguero. That’s OK though, Jesus will continue to bag goals in an understated fashion wherever he ends up. Just remember to put some respect on the man’s name when he’s gone.
