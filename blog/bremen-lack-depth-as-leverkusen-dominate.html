Bremen Lack Depth as Leverkusen Dominate

by Nick Moen - May 18, 2020

| nmoen@umich.edu


Final Score: Werder Bremen 4-1 Bayer Leverkusen

Bremen continued to show their lack of depth and mentality as Leverkusen dominated them in a 4-1 win. Here are five takeaways:


1. Werder Bremen Lack Command in the Defensive Third

Whether it was not following their marks on set pieces or leaving spaces open on the flanks, Bremen showed a lack of desire to defend. No one took charge—captain Niklas Moisander must speak up if his team wants to avoid regulation. Leverkusen left-back, Daley Sinkgraven, was bombing down the left flank the entire game: keeping possession, putting in crosses, and applying pressure to Bremen’s wingers. Three out of four of Leverkusen’s goals were headers. Fourteen headers have been scored against Bremen this season. Aerial duels have to be sorted out and it's Moisander’s responsibility.


2. Kai Havertz is the Next Michael Ballack

Havertz showed his star power, scoring the first two goals of the match. He has the size, the technical skill, and the consistency to reach the next level. Whether that is a top German club like Bayern or a premier league powerhouse like Liverpool, Havertz has the potential. At 20 years old he can keep up with the Bundesliga's best. No wonder he is the youngest player to 30 goals in the Bundesliga. In recent months he earned the captain’s armband. This role can only propel him into a more competitive team.


3. Havertz is the Star, Aránguiz is the Soul

Charles Aránguiz is a player that is underappreciated in a midfield. Havertz dominates the headlines with his goals and skill but Aránguiz does the dirty work. He consistently keeps possession and makes the simple passes that lead to an attacking play. At 5’7 he reminds me of the likes of N’Golo Kante and brings a sense of calmness to Leverkusen. No wonder Leichester fought for him in 2015. In my opinion, his time on the ball controls the tempo of the game and gives the team a sense of relief in high-pressure situations.


4. Bremen Need a Goalscorer

Although they played a strong Leipzig side and will have more forgiving fixtures later, Mainz looked completely out of their depth. Their defending was appalling and affording the likes of Werner so much space is never a good idea. The whole back-line was very sloppy and late with their challenges and reactions. Mainz defenders seemed like they were casually strolling around when Werner scored his second and Leipzig’s fourth while Poulsen was left completely unmarked for a free header for the second goal. In the end, Müller looked like he’d completely given up when Werner poked in his third goal. The heavy loss leaves Mainz in 15th place hovering above the relegation zone. Having already fired Sandro Schwarz earlier in the season, Mainz will be expecting a lot from Achim Beierlorzer to keep them in the Bundesliga.


5. Football is Back but...Different

Whether it is the substitutes wearing face masks or coaches sitting three seats apart on the bench, strange times are upon us. Cameras even cut away from the goal celebrations as Leverkusen abandoned the idea of social distancing. Being in close contact with their opponents throughout the match makes it confusing why embracing each other after a goal is shunned. However, soccer in Germany is the main form of entertainment—especially during this pandemic. The teams and facilitators are trying to promote a positive message and for that and because of that, these regulations should be valued.
