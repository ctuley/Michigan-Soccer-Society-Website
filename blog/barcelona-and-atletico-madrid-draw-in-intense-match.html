Barcelona and Atletico Madrid Draw in Intense Match

by Charlie Tuley - June 30, 2020

ctuley@umich.edu source: Getty Images


Final Score: Barcelona 2 - 2 Atletico Madrid

Barcelona virtually ended their title race with Real Madrid after drawing with Atletico Madrid in the Camp Nou.


1. Setien Rolls Out a New Formation

On paper Setien’s boys seemed to be starting in a 4-4-2 (ter Stegen, Alba-Pique-Lenglet-Semedo, Puig-Rakatic-Busquets-Vidal, Messi-Suarez). However, as soon as the match began, Barcelona took hold of the ball and held most of the possession (72%). When they had possession, they tried to beat Atletico on the wings using a very unconventional 2-3-4-1 (Pique-Lenglet, Rakatic-Busquets-Vidal, Alba-Puig-Messi-Semedo, Suarez). This actually worked quite well for them, as their first goal came from a corner that was earned from an intercepted Messi pass from out wide, and they drew their penalty after Nelson Semedo was brought down after a breakaway on the right-wing.


2. Yannick Carrasco is the MOTM

The Belgian returned to Atleti on loan in January, as they needed an explosive player to help them retain a Champions League spot in La Liga. Even though he hasn’t had the best statistics since his return (1 goal and 2 assists in 11 appearances) he has boosted Atletico’s quality tremendously by providing creativity in the midfield. In their match against Barcelona, Carrasco was undoubtedly the Man of the Match, as he was instrumental in Atleti’s build-up, and he drew both of their penalties.

3. Neither Teams’ Forwards Find the Back Of the Net in Open Play

Before the match, if you had told me that not one of Diego Costa, Yannick Carrasco, Luis Suarez and Lionel Messi would score from open play on Tuesday night, I would have called you a liar. But this turned out to be reality, as three out of the four goals in the match were penalties, and the fourth was an own goal (ironically, courtesy of Diego Costa). While Barcelona and Atletico managed a combined 8 shots on target (5 for Barca, 3 from Atletico), neither team could grab a winner without help from the referee or their opponents’ defenders.


4. Messi Nets his 700th Career Goal

Messi scored his 699th career goal against Leganes on June 16th, and he had to wait two weeks to finally bag number 700. He is now the 7th player to join the 700-club, along with the likes of Cristiano Ronaldo, Pele, and Ferenc Puskas among others. Even though Ronaldo reached 700 goals first, it took the Argentine 111 fewer games to reach this milestone than his eternal rival. Messi now sits on 22 goals this season, and Barcelona fans will be counting on him to continue to carry their team if they want any chance at a La Liga title this season.


5. The Title Race is Just About Over

After their draw Tuesday night, Barcelona now sit one point behind Real Madrid, and Los Blancos have a game in hand. If they beat Getafe on Thursday, they will sit 4 points above their rivals, with only four games remaining. As Madrid have an easy schedule to finish the season (and they can lose a match and still be ahead of Barcelona), it is all but settled that they will win the title. Barca fans will be praying that Madrid slip up in their next two against Getafe and Athletic Club, two very respectable teams.
