Dortmund Score Three in Second Half, Take Down Schalke

by Jake Mozarsky - October 25, 2020

| mozarsky@umich.edu


Final Score: Borussia Dortmund 3-0 FC Schalke

After Bayern Munich and RB Leipzig won their respective matches on Saturday, Dortmund had to defeat rivals FC Schalke in order to maintain their place in the Bundesliga title race. While they started a bit slow, Dortmund was able to put up three goals in the second half in order to win in the Revierderby. Here are five key takeaways:


1. Erling Haaland Continues To Be A Scoring Machine

What Haaland is doing every game is starting to get ridiculous. He has five goals in five Bundesliga games and two assists, and his finishing in front of goal is already one of the best out there. Haaland has already established himself as one of the best scorers in the Bundesliga, and maybe even the world. Maybe a future golden boy?


2. Jadon Sancho Should Stay

Jadon Sancho was one of the hottest names on the transfer market this past summer, attracting the likes of Manchester United, Chelsea, and even Real Madrid. But, Sancho stayed at Dortmund, and maybe that is a good thing. The black and yellow are a young team with stars like him, Haaland, and Gio Reyna, and they might be able to make some noise in the upcoming years if he stays.


3. Bayern Munich Are Just A Step Above

Bayern is now making five key takeaways without playing a game? Yeah, makes sense for the best team in the world. The way Dortmund handled Schalke is something Bayern would laugh at, defeating the blue 8-0 in their first game of the season. Dortmund plays Bayern in two weeks, and that will be an interesting game, as the black and yellow are still outmatched.


4. Schalke Are In Trouble

It was not a long time ago that Schalke were one of the top clubs in Germany. Now, they are 17th in the Bundesliga with only a tie and a loss. Nothing has gone well for the former German contender, and the departures of Weston McKennie, Thilo Kehrer and other names have only caused the club to go to the bottom of the league.

This should not reflect his performance in the Bundesliga, as he has been effective so far, but with a rivalry game against Bayern coming up, hopefully he will not disappear. They need him.
