“New” Chelsea Smash Burnley at Turf Moor

by Jake Mozarsky - November 2, 2020

| mozarsky@umich.edu source: weaintgotnohistory.sbnation.com


Final Score: Burnley 0-3 Chelsea FC

Coming off a 4-0 win in the Champions League against Krasnodar, Chelsea needed a win to climb up the Premier League Table. Frank Lampard rolled out a new formation, and the Blues were able to control every aspect of Saturday’s game at Turf Moor in order to stay within striking distance of the title. Here are five key takeaways:


1. Frank Lampard’s New Formation Works

Lampard went with a 4-3-3 with two attacking midfielders instead of a 4-2-3-1 on Saturday. Mason Mount and Kai Havertz occupied the two attacking midfield roles and both were spectacular, as their mobility and play making they were hard to stop. N’Golo Kante was also able to slide into his preferred position, and nobody on Burnley could get by him. When Christian Pulisic is healthy, slide him on the left wing, put Timo Werner up top and other teams will have trouble defending the Blues.


2. Hakim Ziyech is a Wizard

Two starts for Ziyech, two goals, two Man of the Matches. Ziyech said that he is loving life at Chelsea and nobody blames him. He fits into Lampard’s system and his left foot is almost impossible to stop. He also set up Werner for the third goal Saturday. Ziyech is magical whenever he touches the ball and hopefully he can keep it up for Chelsea.


3. Chelsea Have Found Their Center-Back Pairing

Thiago Silva and Kurt Zouma are the answer for Chelsea, at least for the short term. They have been playing extremely well together and have not given up a goal, and whether or not that is because of Edouard Mendy or not is up to the spectators. Silva is on a one-year deal and it would be advised to extend him as he is helping Kurt Zouma develop into a star. source: standard.co.uk


4. Burnley’s Striker Pair is Overrated

Ashley Barnes and Chris Wood are two big forwards who are supposed to create havoc for every team’s back line. Other than a scare in the 5th minute Saturday, they were nonexistent. Barnes and wood cannot produce well against good teams and are not as skilled as advertised. source: ladbrokes.com


5. Chelsea Have a Forward Dilemma

Chelsea have three forwards that can all make the case to play on any given day. Timo Werner is of course the starter, but Tammy Abraham has been at the top of his form and Olivier Giroud came in yesterday. He also played well, and he almost scored a goal to prove it. Lampard is going to have to stick with one forward when they play great teams, and that will probably be Werner, but Giroud and Abraham are making great cases for starting roles. All I can say to the Premier League is “Watch Out”
