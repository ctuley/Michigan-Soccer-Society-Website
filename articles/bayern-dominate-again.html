Bayern Dominate Again...

by Mitchel Green - June 7, 2020

| mitchgr@umich.edu


Final Score: Bayer 04 Leverkusen 2 - 4 Bayern Münich

Bayern Munich traveled to Bayer Leverkusen this Saturday, and despite being in a losing position for the first time since December, Bayern ultimately took control and won the game 4-2. Here are five key takeaways:


1. Bayern are Unstoppable

If the Champions League somehow returns, Bayern Munich would absolutely be the favorites to win the whole thing. Before today, they hadn’t been in a losing position during a match since the Werder Bremen match in December, and they haven’t been beaten on expected goals since the November match against Frankfurt (fbref.com). They are healthy, they have great depth, they create a lot of chances, and they suffocate their opponents. I don’t see anyone stopping them in the Bundesliga, and I couldn’t see anyone stopping them in Europe either.


2. Leverkusen Struggle to Create Without Havertz

It’s always a struggle to create good chances against Bayern anyway, but without their biggest creative and goalscoring threat, Leverkusen never stood a chance. Kai Havertz was out of the game with a muscle injury he picked up last weekend. In his place was Lucas Alario - who to his credit got on the scoresheet. Unfortunately, after that first goal, Leverkusen struggled to create much of anything and failed to keep Bayern at bay. The expected goals at the end of the match ended 2.6-1.0 in favor of Bayern (fbref.com). Alario did fine as a replacement striker, but he failed to create anything for his teammates - something that Havertz has done so well when in that role. 0 shot-creating actions, 0 key passes, only 13 completed passes, Alario failed to get his teammates involved, and that no doubt contributed to Leverkusen’s struggles.


3. Thomas Müller: Record Breaker


Just as Kevin De Bruyne himself might break the Premier League assist record,Thomas Müller has tied De Bruyne’s Bundesliga assist record. The 30-year-old attacking midfielder has had an incredible season so far, with 7 goals and 20 assists to his name. The amazing thing is that he’s reached the 20 assist mark in about 1000 fewer minutes than De Bruyne did. His expected assists of 11.4 this season suggest he’s been a bit lucky, but when you’re creating chances for players like Robert Lewandowski, your chances are bound to become goals more often than expected. The other amazing thing is that Müller isn’t on set pieces either. Players like De Bruyne can sometimes boost their assist and expected assist numbers by creating chances through set pieces, but Müller creates almost all of his chances via open play. He’s never been particularly flashy, but Thomas Müller has always been able to get the job done.
